  \begin{multicols}{2}
  \topic{zblue}{Emission\pnt{1}}{%
    Zerocracy sends zolds from its Root Wallet to the Office Wallet
    where they can be \href{https://blog.zold.io/2018/12/09/btc-to-zld.html}{purchased} by the Buyers.}
  \topic{zblue}{Buy to Gain\pnt{2}}{%
    The Buyer sends bitcoins to the public BTC Wallet and receives
    zolds to their own ZLD wallet;
    the intent of the Buyer is to gain profit from selling zolds later.}
  \topic{zblue}{Venture Capital\pnt{3}}{%
    A Strategic Investor purchases shares of stock of Zerocracy with an intent
    to sell them later, when the capitalization of the company grows;
    or to earn dividends.}
  \topic{zblue}{Donations\pnt{4}}{%
    In order to increase the exchange rate of ZLD to BTC, Zerocracy donates a portion
    of strategic investment received into the BTC Wallet; the rest of the money
    is spent on business development, R\&D, and marketing.}
  \topic{zblue}{Sell to Return\pnt{5}}{%
    The Seller, when the exchange rate is high enough, converts
    their zolds to bitcoins;
    the total amount of bitcoins in Zerocracy's posession divided by the total
    emission of zolds is the current exchange rate.}
  \topic{zblue}{Exchange Fees\pnt{6}}{%
    In each selling transaction the Seller pays an exchange fee,
    which is accumulated in the Fees Wallet;
    Hosters, who run the network, are getting regular
    \href{https://blog.zold.io/2018/08/14/hosting-bonuses.html}{bonuses}
    from this wallet, on top of
    \href{https://blog.zold.io/2019/01/15/taxes-explained.html}{taxes}.}
  \end{multicols}
  \end{multicols}
